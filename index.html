Grasp Theory is a Personal Content Management System

The Grasp Theory project started over five years ago with one goal, to pursue ways to create positive feedback loops for those who want to improve their own personal knowledge.

This is being achieved by fusing research in cognitive neurocience, education, and mathematics with applications of modern technologies in user interface development, information storage/retrieval, and Machine Learning.

The first prototype will be complete in early 2018.

Shoot us an email to join our mailing list for updates.

Some key principals followed while the project has developed:

  • Hebian learning is fundamental. Leverage associations between related content. Context is king!
  • Leverage the brain's large visual bandwidth and spacial encoding abilities.
  • Be deliberate in assisting the construction and analysis the underlying graph of information generated by the users.

Is Grasp Theory "open source"

The software aspects of the project are currently not open source as it is still in major flux. The long term goal, however, is to open source the code once the direction solidfies. We are communicating information to many people and appreciate the feedback from the community that further drives this project.

Are you building the "MEMEX"?

YES! We had not actually heard of the "MEMEX"" as proposed by Vannevar Bush until Tim Strehle brought it to our attention.

Vannevar Bush headed the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development and published an article in 1945 that described his desire for a system to manage knowledge. It gives us chills to realize he envisioned exactly what we are working on (to a large degree) 65 years before we got our momentum.

Many articles that site the 'MEMEX' say his visions were realized as modern 'hypertext' or the internet itself. If you re-read his article you will see he has a large emphasis on the visual/spacial apsects of knowledge. Grasp Theory uses a LOT more than micro films but maintains the emphasis on exploiting the brains high visual and spacial bandwidth/reasoning.

If only Vannevar Bush had access to WEBGL, SVG, D3, NEO4J, and a multitude of other technolgies. We hope we do him proud! He envisioued our proudcut 65 years ago before computers even existed! His protoge was Claude Shannon, the father of Information Theory. We have big shoes to fill and are humbled to pursue and enhance his vision.
