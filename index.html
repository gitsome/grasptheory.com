LEARN WHAT YOU KNOW 

Search, Create, and Learn like Never Before

The goal of the Grasp Theory project is to help users gain more insight into their digital content.

This is being achieved by fusing research in Cognitive Neurocience, Education, and Mathematics with applications of modern technologies in user interface development, information storage/retrieval, and Machine Learning.

The first prototype will be complete in early 2018.

Send an email to join the mailing list for updates.

Some key principals followed while the project has developed:

  • Hebian learning is fundamental. Leverage associations between related content. Context is king.
  • Leverage the brain's large visual bandwidth and spacial encoding abilities.
  • Be deliberate in assisting the construction and analysis of the underlying graph of information generated by the user.

Are you building the "MEMEX"?

YES! We had not actually heard of the "MEMEX"" as proposed by Vannevar Bush until Tim Strehle brought it to our attention.

Vannevar Bush headed the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development and published an article in 1945 that described his desire for a system to manage knowledge that he dubbed the "MEMEX."

Many articles that site the 'MEMEX' say his visions were realized as modern 'hypertext' or the internet itself. If you re-read his article you will see he has a large emphasis on the visual/spacial apsects of knowledge. Grasp Theory uses a LOT more than micro films but maintains the emphasis on exploiting the brains high visual and spacial bandwidth/reasoning.

His protoge was Claude Shannon, the father of Information Theory. We have big shoes to fill and are humbled to incorporate and enhance his vision.
